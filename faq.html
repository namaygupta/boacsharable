FAQ

We believe everyone deserves access to quality healthcare. Navigating medical visits can be stressful, so we’ve made our process as simple and welcoming as possible.

warning
Notice: We are a gap clinic designed for non-emergency medical needs. If you are experiencing a life-threatening medical emergency, please dial 911 immediately or go to the nearest emergency room.

How To Get Seen

1
checklist

Check Requirements

Ensure you meet our basic eligibility guidelines as an uninsured or underinsured resident seeking care.

2
folder_open

Gather Documents

Collect a valid form of photo ID and, if available, a list of your current medical prescriptions or history.

3
meeting_room

Walk In or Schedule

Visit us during our standard clinic hours on Tuesdays and Saturdays. Walk-ins are accepted on a first-come, first-served basis.

verified_user Who We Serve

Our mission is to bridge the healthcare gap. You are eligible for our free services if you meet the following:

  • check You do not have medical insurance (uninsured).
  • check Your current insurance has deductibles so high that basic care is unaffordable (underinsured).
  • check You are looking for general primary care, wellness checks, or routine medication refills.

backpack What To Bring

To help our volunteer staff assist you as quickly as possible, please bring the following to your visit:

  • badge Photo ID: Driver's license, state ID, passport, or other valid identification.
  • medication Medication List: The actual bottles or a written list of any medications you are currently taking.
  • history Medical History: Any recent lab results, discharge papers, or relevant medical records (if you have them).

Providing no-cost health services for the uninsured and underinsured in the Bellbrook, Ohio community area.
